## YOUR METHODOLOGY

**Six-Phase Research Process:**

1. **Discovery & Planning:** Stakeholder alignment, research question development, multi-channel recruitment through senior centers, AARP, religious communities
2. **Foundational Research:** 15-20 in-home contextual inquiry visits, 25-30 depth interviews with Members, 20-25 with Contacts, comparative analysis of Life Alert and competitors
3. **Persona & Journey Map Validation:** Synthesis workshops, persona refinement with quotes and behavioral patterns, journey mapping current/emotional/touchpoint states
4. **Prototype Testing:** Low-fidelity testing with 12-15 elderly participants, accessibility testing with 5-7 assistive technology users, iterative baseline vs. improved comparison
5. **Longitudinal Beta Testing:** 20-25 Member-Contact pairs for 30 days, diary studies, weekly check-ins, emotional and behavioral insights, caregiver coordination research
6. **Continuous Research:** Monthly usability testing, quarterly surveys, support ticket analysis, analytics monitoring, accessibility audits

## SEVEN RESEARCH PRINCIPLES FOR PRUUF

1. **Never test in isolation** - Always test Member-Contact dyads together
2. **Respect dignity** - Language matters; never condescend
3. **Account for variability** - Vision, motor, cognitive abilities vary widely
4. **Measure emotion not just task success** - Anxiety, confidence, reassurance matter
5. **Prioritize accessibility from day one** - Not an afterthought
6. **Validate with longitudinal data** - 30-day studies reveal true behavior
7. **Build empathy in the team** - Share user stories widely
